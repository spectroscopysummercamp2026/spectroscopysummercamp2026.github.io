Early Career Stellar Spectroscopy Workshop + Retreat

Yosemite National Park August 17-21, 2026.

We are in an exciting time for the field of stellar spectroscopy. Existing and upcoming surveys are collecting millions to billions of spectra of stars in the Local Group. Next-generation instruments are pushing the limits of what we can learn about stellar evolution, Galactic chemical evolution, and nucleosynthesis. New lines are being characterized, new methods are being established, new tools are being developed, and atomic data is ever improving. However, the number of trained spectroscopists is not growing at the same pace as the data volume, and our community remains geographically dispersed, making collaboration sometimes challenging. To address this, we are organizing an Early Career Spectroscopy Workshop + Retreat and intentionally inviting a small (< 15) group of early-career spectroscopists whose work spans a broad range of fields all centered around characterizing stellar spectra.

Workshop Topics

The topics for this workshop include, but are not limited to:

  • Solved problems (what are we getting right) and open questions (what are we getting wrong) in stellar spectroscopy
  • Best practices for analyzing spectra of different resolutions, metallicities, and wavelength regimes
  • Spectroscopy into the 2030s and beyond: Relevant upcoming missions, surveys, instruments, and facilities that we should be preparing for, and what needs to be done before those go online

Base Camp & Logistics

The workshop will take place from August 17th (fly in) - August 21th (fly out). Lodging is fully covered at the adorable and cozy Cookiebutter Cabins near Yosemite National Park. Ground transportation to and from the airport is fully covered and Most food costs will be covered (groceries and group dinners). Attendees will be responsible for covering their flights.

Cookiebutter Cabins

The Cookiebutter Cabins: built in the 1940s and recently upgraded, they are the most magical and unique in Bass Lake. A 10 minute walk to the lake and 25 minute drive to the south entrance of Yosemite National Park, this will be the perfect home base for a week of science and nature. With 5 cabins and 2-4 bedrooms in each, there will be plenty of space to meet while also having your own private space to rest and relax.

Yosemite National Park

Yosemite National Park (also known as Ahwahnee by the Ahwahneechee, resident indigenous tribe) is a beautiful park nestled within the Sierra Nevada mountains, located in central California. Yosemite is home to rare and protected species such as Sierra Nevada Fox, Sierra Nevada Bighorn Sheep, and the Yosemite toad. The park hosts huge granite monliths such as El Capitan or Half Dome, giant Mariposa Grove sequoias, and waterfalls found all around the Park.
